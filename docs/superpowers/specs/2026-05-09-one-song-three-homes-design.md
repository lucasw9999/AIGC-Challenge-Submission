**Project:** Lucas's 3-minute film for the 2026 CIE/USA-DFW Student AIGC Competition

**Submission deadline:** May 15, 2026 · 11:59 PM CDT

## 1. Premise

A 9th-grade violinist living in the Bay Area packs his violin and returns to Sunrise at FlatIrons (Broomfield, CO) — the retirement community he volunteered at during his Colorado years — to play *Molihua* (茉莉花, Jasmine Flower) for the elders. With every phrase of the song, painted memories of small kindnesses from earlier in his life — Australia, China — unfold on screen. He ends the song. The room is silent. One elder wipes her eyes.

*Small acts, received across oceans, returned in a single song.*

**Creative-framing note:** The act of "traveling back" is a mild dramatic device that ties Lucas's four life locations (Australia, China, Colorado, Bay Area) together in one film. The underlying volunteer work at Sunrise at FlatIrons is real. Rules permit imaginative treatment of real experience.

## 3. Authenticity compliance

The competition rules state: *"All stories must be based on the participant's real volunteer or community service experience. Purely fictional stories are not allowed."*

**How this design complies:**

- The central volunteer act — Lucas playing violin for seniors at **Sunrise at FlatIrons, 400 Summit Boulevard, Broomfield, CO 80021** — is his **real, past volunteer work** during the family's Boulder years.
- The framing device of "returning" from the Bay Area is mild dramatic interpretation of real service — it compresses Lucas's real journey and real volunteer work into one arc. The volunteer work itself is unambiguously real.
- The **memories** are Lucas's inner life during the performance — memories of kindnesses he himself received that motivate him to give back.
- The film **ends on an explicit text card** naming the real place: *"Based on Lucas's real volunteer work playing violin at Sunrise at FlatIrons, Broomfield CO."*
- The two memory vignettes (Australia, China) each require a **real-life seed** (even a small one). Placeholders are proposed in §6 and must be confirmed or replaced with real family stories before production.

## 5. Visual style

**Overall direction:** "Painted Memory." The Bay Area home, Colorado arrival, and Sunrise at FlatIrons interior are rendered with semi-realistic warmth (golden hour, shallow depth of field). Each memory vignette uses a distinct painted treatment:

- **Australia — Watercolor.** Sun-drenched pastel palette, visible brushstrokes, loose edges. Felt-memory, impressionistic.
- **China — Ink-wash (shui-mo).** Monochrome with a single red accent, jasmine petals, calligraphic strokes. Steam, tea, hands.
- **Present-day (Bay Area / transit / Sunrise at FlatIrons) — Semi-realistic.** Slightly saturated to feel emotionally amplified; no heavy stylization.

**Transitions** stitch the styles via single unifying motifs: a jasmine petal drifting from one world to the next, a bow stroke dissolving into a brush stroke, sunlight refracting through violin strings into ink.

**Reference inspirations (for mood boards, not pastiche):** *Loving Vincent*, *The Red Turtle*, Ghibli memory sequences, ink-wash moments in *Kung Fu Panda*, Chloé Zhao's golden-hour realism.

## 7. Audio

- **Hero:** Solo violin rendition of *Molihua* (茉莉花). Melody is public domain.
- **Preferred:** Capture a 30–60-second phone recording of Lucas performing *Molihua*. This provides:
  - Real authentic human audio core
  - Zero licensing risk
  - A distinctive, personal sound unavailable to other competitors
- **Fallback:** Royalty-free violin recording of *Molihua*, properly credited.
- **Diegetic ambience per scene:** quiet home morning (Bay Area opening), distant jet hum and wind (transit), warm murmurs and floor creak (Sunrise at FlatIrons), seagulls/breeze (Australia memory), soft tea pour and brush on paper (China memory), sustained single violin note into silence (return and final beat).
- **No voice-over.** The film earns its feeling through music and image.

## 9. AI production approach

- **Model:** Google Veo3, accessed through the Veo 3.1 cloud API.
- **Shot granularity:** ~10–14 shots total, each ~5–10 seconds. Stitched in a video editor (iMovie, DaVinci Resolve, or CapCut).
- **Style consistency strategy:**
  - One curated reference mood board per vignette
  - Standardized style phrases appended to every prompt in a given vignette ("watercolor, visible brushstrokes, pastel palette, soft edges, golden light" etc.)
  - Generate multiple takes per shot; pick best for edit
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9, 1080p or 4K depending on Veo3 output capabilities (to be confirmed in the Veo 3.1 capability investigation)
- **Graceful degradation:** If a shot cannot be achieved via text-to-video, fall back to (a) image-to-video from a still, (b) a generated still with subtle Ken Burns motion, or (c) simplify the shot.
- **Veo 3.1 API / Veo3 capability investigation is a prerequisite task** — see §11.

## 12. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Veo3 cannot generate a specific shot convincingly | Graceful degradation to image-to-video or still with motion; painted styles hide imperfections |
| Ran out of API quota / rate limited | Build shot list early; generate in priority order (hero shots first); have fallback shots designed in |
| Faces look uncanny in realistic retirement-home frame | Lean on hands, bow, gestures, profiles, out-of-focus audience — avoid unfamiliar tight face close-ups except for the final elder-tear moment, where we pick the best of many takes |
| Authenticity challenged by judges | Closing card names real volunteer work; each memory is confirmed to have a real seed; documentation of Lucas's participation kept |
| Real violin audio unavailable | Fallback to public-domain royalty-free *Molihua* |
| Over-length video | Hard-cut to 2:55 target; 3:00 upper bound; trim closing card if needed |
| Style drift between shots | Per-vignette style guide prompts; reference image re-use; batch-regenerate inconsistent shots |
