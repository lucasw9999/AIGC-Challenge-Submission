**Student:** Lucas · Grade 9 · Division C

**Theme:** *Small Acts, Big Impact*
**Film:** `deliverable/one-song-three-homes-v9.mp4` (2:27, 1080p, H.264 + AAC)

## Summary

I made this 3-minute film from my real volunteer work — playing violin for the seniors at Sunrise at FlatIrons in Broomfield, Colorado. The story is about how kindness travels: from strangers who helped me as a child in Australia, China, and Colorado, back out to the elders I play for today.

- **Two AI models working together.** **Claude Opus 4.7** is the orchestrating AI — it writes the prompts, calls **Google Veo 3.1** through a cloud API for each shot, and then handles all the editing and assembly itself. Two different AI models, one doing creative coordination, the other generating video.
- **16 video shots** (1080p, 8 seconds each) generated by **Google Veo 3.1**, including lip-synced dialogue in English and Mandarin and baked-in ambient audio
- **1 violin soundtrack** — *Molihua* (茉莉花) — synthesized from a public-domain MIDI after AI-verified melody matching
- **Creative direction by me:** the real story, the narrative choices, the casting, and the artistic review of every cut. I fully leveraged AI to handle the technical production so I could focus my time on what the film was trying to say.
- **9 numbered iterations** from first cut to submission
- **Clean licensing**: public-domain melody + public-domain MIDI + MIT-licensed SoundFont + AI-original visuals

### Two AI models, working together

Two different AI models played different roles:

- **Claude Opus 4.7** was my orchestrator. It wrote the prompts, called the video-generation model, handled the music, and did all the editing and assembly. I talked to it in plain English; it did the work.
- **Google Veo 3.1** was the video-generation model. For each shot, the orchestrating AI sent a carefully written prompt with casting, wardrobe, scene, lighting, camera, and dialogue. Veo 3.1 returned an 8-second 1080p clip with lip-synced voices and baked-in ambient audio.

After all 16 clips came back from Veo 3.1, the orchestrating AI did the stitching itself — concatenating shots, mixing the Veo-generated ambient and dialogue with the *Molihua* violin track, rendering the title and closing cards, slowing the final bow into a cinematic hold, and producing the final MP4. One script (`scripts/build_film.sh`) reproduces the whole film from source.

## How the process ran

### Phase 1 — Brainstorming (Superpowers plugin · `brainstorming` skill)

I opened a session with Claude Opus 4.7 and shared the competition URL. The AI:
- Fetched the rules and surfaced the authenticity requirement (no fully-fictional stories)
- Asked structured questions about my real life one at a time
- Proposed three candidate narrative structures with trade-offs; I chose "One Song, Three Homes"
- Proposed three candidate visual tones; I chose photorealistic cinematic
- Wrote a full design specification that I reviewed and approved

### Phase 2 — Planning (Superpowers plugin · `writing-plans` skill)

The AI converted the approved design into an executable implementation plan covering: project bootstrap, cloud API capability probe, mood boards, shot list, smoke tests, full generation, audio production, editing, verification, and submission prep.

### Phase 3 — Execution (Superpowers plugin · `subagent-driven-development` skill)

The AI dispatched **specialized subagents**, one per task, each with exactly the context it needed. Subagents ran in parallel where possible — while one was generating a memory vignette, another was sourcing the *Molihua* MIDI, another was building the ffmpeg pipeline. The main conversation stayed focused on my creative review instead of mechanics.

**Tasks executed by subagents:** project bootstrap, cloud API capability probe, prompt file authoring, mood-board sourcing, 16 individual shot generations, audio sourcing and synthesis, video assembly, text-card rendering, final muxing.

### Phase 4 — Generation (Veo 3.1 cloud API)

Each of the 16 shots was individually generated by the AI calling Veo 3.1:

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Model | `veo-3.1-generate-001` |
| Resolution | 1080p (1920 × 1080) |
| Duration | 8 seconds (Veo 3.1's max per clip) |
| Aspect | 16:9 |
| Audio | AAC stereo 48 kHz baked in (dialogue + ambient) |
| `enhancePrompt` | true (Veo 3.1 requires it) |
| `personGeneration` | allow_all |
| `sampleCount` | 1 per shot |
| Negative prompt | used on every shot |

**Every voice in the film is Veo-generated:** the preschool teacher's English, the stranger's Mandarin in the market, the ski coach's encouragement. They're lip-synced to the characters the prompt described.

### Phase 5 — Music (AI-sourced + AI-synthesized)

The AI:
1. Searched public-domain audio archives
2. Found a public-domain MIDI transcription of *Molihua* on Wikimedia Commons
3. **Verified the melody by note-interval analysis** — the MIDI's opening sequence (F#4 F#4 A4 B4 D5) matches the canonical Molihua melody transposed up a major third, and the antecedent–consequent repeat at bar 5 matches the Molihua verse structure
4. Synthesized a solo-violin rendering with fluidsynth + FluidR3 GM SoundFont (MIT)
5. Cleaned and normalized the audio with ffmpeg (high-pass filter, dynamic range, −18 LUFS target)

**Output:** `audio/molihua-main.wav` (2:40 solo violin Molihua)

### Phase 6 — Editing (AI-authored pipeline)

ffmpeg-based, Python-orchestrated, fully reproducible with one command (`scripts/build_film.sh`):

1. Normalize each shot to 1920×1080 / 24 fps
2. Concatenate shots in film order per `edit/concat-list.txt`
3. Render text cards (title, interstitial, closing) in Python + Pillow
4. Build two-layer audio master:
   - **Foreground:** Veo-baked ambient + dialogue per shot (loudnorm: −28 LUFS for opening, −20 LUFS for memories, −30 LUFS for return)
   - **Background:** Molihua layer at 60% volume
5. Apply slow-motion to final shot (setpts=2×) for a held closing gesture
6. Fade to black, mux final MP4

### Phase 7 — Iteration

I watched every cut. Each round, I named specific things that were not yet right — a fake-looking tear, the wrong helper ethnicity, a physically incorrect bow direction, too many words on the closing card. The AI re-wrote the relevant prompts, re-generated the affected shots, rebuilt the audio mix, and delivered a new numbered version.

Each version is preserved in `deliverable/` so nothing was ever lost:
- **v1** — first rough cut from existing generations
- **v2** — music timing sync, Veo ambient added
- **v3** — all-photorealistic restart, stronger kindness stories
- **v4** — dialogue and ambient audio layered in
- **v5** — natural tears, Black helper consistency, parents come to me, photo reference attempt
- **v6** — Asian helper (from v4) + snowplow coaching (from v5) mixed
- **v7** — slow-mo bow, shorter closing card text
- **v8** — trimmed the last bad bow second
- **v9** — final: trimmed again for clean bow throughout, 2:27 total (submission)

**Each iteration was a specific direct-and-execute loop.** I described what I wanted and the AI updated the pipeline.

## What I learned

- **Writing prompts is writing.** The creative quality of each 8-second shot lives in the 250 words of its prompt. Vague prompts = vague results.
- **Each 8-second shot should be one sustained moment.** Multi-beat prompts ("first X happens, then Y, then Z") are silently interpreted by Veo 3.1 as mini-montages. One held gesture, sustained for eight seconds, is the right unit.
- **Style needs intention, not decoration.** One consistent photorealistic look with a subtle warm haze on memories landed better than any attempt at mixing painted/stylized visuals.
- **Music is the spine.** Holding *Molihua* across every vignette is what makes the film feel unified instead of like three separate stories.
- **AI amplifies intention; it does not replace it.** The AI can do thousands of things at production quality. It cannot know when the story is right. That stays with me.
