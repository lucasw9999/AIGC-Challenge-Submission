*One Song, Three Homes* is about how kindness travels. I play violin for the seniors at Sunrise at FlatIrons in Broomfield, Colorado — a small act of service. As I play *Molihua*, the film unfolds three moments from my childhood when strangers helped me: a teacher at my Australian preschool who welcomed me in, an adult who found me when I was lost in a Chinese market, and a skier who lifted me up after a hard fall in Colorado. Every small act I received lives inside my song now.

I used AI to share this story because it let me make something at a scale I couldn't reach alone — generating 16 cinematic shots with dialogue and ambient sound, synthesizing the music, and assembling the film — so I could focus on the story itself.
