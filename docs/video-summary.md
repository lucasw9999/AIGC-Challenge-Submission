*One Song, Three Homes* is about how kindness travels. I play violin for the seniors at Sunrise at FlatIrons in Broomfield, Colorado — a small act of service. As I play Molihua, the film unfolds three times strangers were kind to me as a child: a teacher at my Australian preschool who welcomed me in, an adult who helped me when I was lost in a Chinese market, and a stranger skier who lifted me up after a hard fall in Colorado. Every small act I received lives in my song now.

I used AI to share this story because it let me make something at a scale I couldn't reach alone — generating 16 cinematic shots with dialogue and ambient sound, synthesizing the violin, and assembling the film — while I focused on the story itself: the memories, the music, and the feeling each moment should carry.
